# Social Media Checklist

## Do’s

1. Think before posting anything online.
2. Keep your account privacy settings enabled.
3. Use strong passwords and update them regularly.
4. Be respectful and polite in comments and messages.
5. Verify information before sharing it.

## Don’ts

1. Do not share personal information like phone number, address, or passwords.
2. Avoid posting offensive or hurtful content.
3. Do not accept friend requests from strangers.
4. Don’t click on suspicious links or unknown attachments.
5. Avoid oversharing your location or daily routine.

## Scenario of Poor Communication

A student posted a sarcastic comment on a friend’s photo, intending it as a joke. However, the tone was misunderstood, and the friend felt hurt and embarrassed. This led to an argument and affected their friendship.

This situation could be improved by choosing words carefully and avoiding sarcasm online, as tone is often difficult to interpret in text. It is better to communicate respectfully and, if joking, ensure the other person understands the intent. If a misunderstanding occurs, apologizing quickly and clarifying the message can help resolve the issue and maintain healthy relationships.
